Dear mjt,
I’ve rewritten this page many times,
because I didn’t know how to explain my silence
without sounding broken.

When university started, I wasn’t doing well —
not in a dramatic way,
but in a quiet, confusing way where I didn’t recognize myself anymore.
I was afraid to let you see me like that.
You knew me when I was confident, energetic, and full of plans.
I was scared that if I showed you who I had become,
I would disappoint you —
or worse, lose the version of myself that only existed in your eyes.

So I chose silence.
Not because I forgot you,
but because I missed you too much.

This semester hasn’t been about success.
It has been about learning how to stay alive gently —
through books, parks, music, photos, and small moments of peace.

I’m still finding my way.
But I wanted you to know:
you were never left behind.

Thank you for being someone
who once made my days lighter,
and who still lives quietly in my memory.

I hope this small website can tell you something
I didn’t know how to say before:

I’m here.
I’m learning.
And I wish you a warm, kind Christmas.

With love,
[Your Name]
